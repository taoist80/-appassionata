## Laura's Story  (/laura-s-story)
*Title tag: "Music Lessons with Laura Francis"*

**H2:** Laura's Story: My Musical Adventure

Playing songs by ear on a mini electric keyboard, my mom suspected her little girl probably had some talent and would enjoy taking piano lessons (thanks Mom!!) My formal lessons began at 7, one month shy of turning 8. I appreciated how dedicated my parents were to seeing me succeed at piano by investing their encouragement, time, finances, into 14 years of lessons!

In fact my mom sat with me almost everyday those first few years to oversee my practicing and guide me along the way. What an incredible difference that made in my consistent progress and continued motivation to practice.

At 18, I began teaching piano and knew this is what I wanted to do for the rest of my life! The last chapter of my formal music studies brought me to Utah State University where I received a Bachelor of Music cum laude in Piano Pedagogy.

Along with piano study, came cello lessons at age 10 until 22. My Dad bought me my very own cello at 16 (thanks Dad!!) Playing in orchestra through my childhood, during my university years, and now in the Utah Philharmonic Orchestra as principal cellist, is another great love of mine.

I was also greatly inspired by my piano professors Dr. Irene Weiss Peery-Fox and Dr. Dianne Hardy. My cello professors Dr. Warren Burton and Dr. James McWhorter encouraged me immensely during my high school and college years. Extreme gratitude for all my amazing professors for helping to shape me into the musician and teacher I am today!

My musical adventure continues to this day as I begin my 35th year of teaching this September 2025!!

> "To play a wrong note is insignificant; to play without passion is inexcusable." — Ludwig van Beethoven

**PIANO Blast Group Lessons** — Engaging weekly 60 min group lessons designed to spend time learning piano skills, playing by ear, theory games, and learning to read music in a relaxed but structured and encouraging environment. Class size limited to 6 total students. 12 month program, including lessons, Christmas Performance Party, recitals during the school year session. Summer Session is a combination of individual lessons and a music camp. **Monthly tuition $125** includes supplementary music and supplies. (Lesson & theory books and metronome purchased separately after the first 8 weeks.)

Weekly Individual Lessons: limited number of 30, 45 or 60 minute time slots. Monthly 50-minute Performance/Theory Class grouped by similar age/playing level.

**MiniMusic Piano Summer Camp** — A music appreciation camp for children not ready for group piano lessons but wanting to explore an interest in music.

### Students 8–11 Years Old
**Piano or Cello Blast Group Lessons** — Engaging weekly 50 minute group lessons (piano or cello skills, playing by ear, theory games, reading music). Class size limited to 5 total students. **Monthly tuition $125** includes supplementary music and supplies.

**Individual Piano or Cello Lessons & Monthly Performance/Theory Class** — For students who have completed MiniMusic, group lessons, and/or have been taking individual lessons. 30/45/60 minute weekly slots + monthly 50-minute class.

**Summer Mini Camps** — Weekly 90-minute hands-on learning with friends. Themes: composers, musical instruments, music around the world, Musical S.T.E.M., composing, reading lead charts and improvising, and more.

### 11 Years Old – College Age
**Individual Piano Lessons & Monthly Performance/Theory Class** — Weekly 45 or 60 minute slots; monthly 50-minute performance/theory class.

**Individual Cello Lessons & Performance/Theory Class** — Weekly 30/45/60 minute lessons for beginners to adults; great for elementary–high school orchestra students. Theory and ear training included.

**Summer Music Camps & Workshops** — Fun, hands-on learning for elementary/middle school (camps) and high school (workshops).

Looking to start a new hobby or get back into lessons after a long break? Flexible scheduling (daytime and evening). 4-lesson packages of 30/45/60 minute lessons. Individualized instruction to meet interests and goals.

- **Prep for College Music Degree and Auditions** — Planning for serious 9th–12th grade musicians. Contact Laura for tuition.

## MiniMusic Class  (/music-adventures/minimusic-class)
*Title tag: "MiniMusic"*

**H2:** MiniMusic Class!

**Would MiniMusic be a good fit for my child?**
- Interested in giving your child ages 5–7 an opportunity to explore music, instruments, and gain musicianship skills but not ready for individual lessons?
- Do you want them to be excited and confident about learning to play an instrument or take voice lessons while they are still young?
- Not sure if they will even like learning the piano?

If you answered yes to all these questions, sign up for MiniMusic Class first! There is no need to have a piano at home (although if you have one, that is great!) and all learning is done in class…no practicing is required!

**What is MiniMusic?**
- Hands-on educational program designed to provide children ages 5–7 with the foundational knowledge of music theory, music appreciation, and beginning piano skills.
- Fun group experience where learning is done through games, art, literature, singing, listening activities, and much more!
- Concepts taught include rhythm, note values, musical alphabet, and instruments.

**MiniMusic FAQ's**
- **Length:** 12 months of music and piano! School Year Session is 30 weeks / 60-min classes, Christmas Music Party, Graduation Party. Summer Session includes 30-min individual piano lessons and a Music Camp!
- **Cost:** Tuition invoiced in monthly payments of $110.
- **Included in tuition:** Music Activity Book and all supplies/resources!
- **Who:** Children ages 5–7
- **When:** Wednesdays 5:00–6:00 pm
- **Where:** Home Studio in Sandy (block south of Alta View Elementary)
- **Registration:** Non-refundable $25 fee. New families: register on the Home page and book a meet-and-greet lesson before starting class.

## Studio Life and Policy  (/studio-life-and-policy)
*Title tag: "Appassionata Piano & Cello Studio - Studio Life and Policy"* — (Updated July 2025)

This is a long, text-heavy policy page covering parent, teacher, and student responsibilities and studio expectations. Section structure:

1. **Laura's Thoughts: How I feel about music lessons!** — Teacher's philosophy and commitment to each student (sensitivity to learning styles, well-rounded musicianship, patient/encouraging atmosphere, exploring varied genres, building confident musicians for life).
2. **Parents: A key to success!** — Practice support expectations: consistent daily practice time, weekly notebook checks, instrument at home (tuned), correct bench height/metronome/lighting, supervision rules, open communication.
3. **Students: The teacher's expectations!** — Note-reading fluency, theory, technique, ear training, musicality, musicianship, performing; keyboard skills (sight reading, composing, improvising, ensemble). Arrive prepared; practice 5 days/week; have fun.
4. **Tuition: What does it cover?** — All-inclusive: performance/theory classes, parties, camps; teacher education; piano tuning/repairs/maintenance; music library; lesson planning; equipment; supplies; recital venue rentals/programs/rewards; incentive programs; online subscriptions; sheet music. Year-round studio; 12 equal monthly payments based on enrollment (not attendance). Due by the 6th; $25 late fee. Invoiced monthly/quarterly/yearly. Payment by cash, check, Zelle, bank bill pay, PayPal, or Venmo business (PayPal/Venmo fees added). Annual cost-of-living increases possible at year start; non-refundable $25 annual enrollment/scheduling fee.
5. **Summer Session (June–August)** — NO lesson reschedules or credits.
6. **Lesson Attendance: Be consistent!** — 30-day written advance notification to withdraw; one month tuition still due; supervision warning for early drop-off/late pickup.
7. **Need to Miss a Lesson: Simple Steps to follow!** — Log in and cancel attendance on the studio calendar (min 24 hrs notice). Missed-lesson options (student's responsibility to arrange): Skype lessons (mild illness/weather/vacation), same-week reschedule (if available, first come first serve), video lesson recording, composer activity packets / Boom cards. Illness policy: fever-free 24 hrs before in-studio lessons. Group/performance/duo lessons cannot be rescheduled or credited. Teacher-cancelled lessons are rescheduled or credited.
8. **Lesson Evaluation: A valuable tool!** — Annual written/conference evaluation (promptness, technique, theory, preparation, practice, attitude). Lessons may be suspended/terminated for unsatisfactory evaluation, repeated failure to meet goals, lack of parental involvement, or financial delinquency.
9. **Music Library / Digital and Hardcopy Resources / Apps: A BIG Savings for you!** — Borrowable supplementary music (3–5 books/year). Includes: Piano Book Club, SuperSonics, Wunderkeys, Piano Adventures supplements (Disney, Classics, Pop), Piano Pronto, Digital Music Games, Piano Safari, Suzuki, supplementary cello music, Piano Guys / 2Cellos, MuseScore and MusicNotes sheet music.
10. **Performance Opportunities: A time for sharing the gift of music!** — Two required recitals per year (Fall and Spring). Alternatives for non-participation (videos, family recital, public space performance). Performance-anxiety accommodations. Optional competitions, piano festivals, Federation, and AIM achievement exams based on readiness and parent support.
11. **Contact me: I'd love to hear from you!** — Reachable via the studio contact page or by phone/text (801) 455-5539.
